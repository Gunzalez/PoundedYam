Ailing and stuck at home, I thought I'd learn some Angular js, and so I made this app with it, plus a bit of jQuery.

Okay it doesn't look too terrible, sadly it doesn't quite work well either, sniff. However my goal was achieved, I got to learn quite a bit of Angular js, nice!

For best results, try not to scroll up and down too quickly, you'll kill the poor thing and then need to refresh to continue. Don't hate me bro, I was only learning. Bitch, how dare you mock me!

Works fine on a PC browser, but on mobile browsers lord only knows what goes on there. This, my friend is why native will never die, iOS forever!

  • Took me about 2 weeks without any prior Angular js knowledge.
  • Learnt a lot more than what you see here, especially what not to do.
  • Always nice to get your mistakes in early and learn from them, good fun Angular js.
  • I'm still pro native and less so mobile web apps, stuff is just too slow.
  • Relying on the user's bandwidth is like catching a bus - you just never know how long you're going to wait (performance), this is why I cycle.
